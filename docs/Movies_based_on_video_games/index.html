Video Games Make Terrible Movies

By: Julien Assouline





Netflix announced that they were going to produce a TV series based on The Witcher. The books, not the game. This bodes well for Netflix, because there haven’t been many successful movies based off of video games.

One of the latest attempts was turning the Assassin’s Creed video game series into a movie. They even got renowned actors such as Michael Fassbender and Marion Cotillard to star in the movie, and yet it was still a flub. Rotten Tomatoes gave it a rating of 18%.

Assassin's Creed though wasn’t an aberration. It wasn’t just one bad movie out of a batch of good ones. On average Assassins Creed 18% rating was basically the average rating for movies based on video games which stands at 17.9%.



Rotten Tomatoes ratings on movies based from video games

Rotten Tomatoes gives a rating out of 100. Movies with under a rating of 60% are considered “Rotten”. Movies with a rating of 60% or better are considered “Fresh” and movie with a rating of 75 or better are considered “Certified Fresh”.

No movie based on a video game has ever been considered “Fresh” by Rotten Tomatoes. In fact the best rating ever given to a movie based on a video game was “Final Fantasy: The Spirits Within”.

The move, therefore, by Netflix to base the movie on the books rather than the video games seems to be a good one and next time you get excited about a movie, based on a video game, it might be best to step back and lower your expectations, because there’s never been a quality movie of that type.
